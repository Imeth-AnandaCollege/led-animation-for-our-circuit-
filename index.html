How the Circuit Works:

This is a simple simulation of an astable multivibrator circuit with two LEDs. Here's how the circuit works:

  1. Power Supply: The circuit is powered by a voltage source that applies a voltage to the circuit components.
  2. Resistors and Capacitors: The resistors (R1, R2) and capacitors (C1, C2) are responsible for charging and discharging. When power is applied, the capacitors start charging through the resistors.
  3. Transistor Switching: The capacitors charge and discharge, which triggers the transistors (T1, T2). The transistors act as switches that control the LEDs.
  4. LED Blinking: When a transistor turns on, it allows current to flow through the corresponding LED, turning it on. The other LED is turned off as its transistor is in the off state.
  5. Cycle Repeats: As one capacitor fully charges, it switches the corresponding transistor, turning one LED on while turning the other off. The cycle then repeats, causing the LEDs to blink alternately.
